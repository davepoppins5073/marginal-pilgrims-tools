## What Converts on Substack About Pages (Research)

From analysis of high-converting About pages:

1. Answer three questions in the first 200 words: who you are, what you offer, why it matters to them
2. The page is a sales page for the publication, not a vanity page
3. Structure that works: what it is + who it's for → subscribe button → who you are → what readers get → second CTA
4. Two subscribe buttons: one above the fold, one at the bottom
- Social proof (subscriber count, retention signal)
- Specific not generic — name concrete things
- Warm, short, human closing line

**Reference example — David Coggins (The Contender):**
His About page is ~200 words. Opens with three named books and publisher (Scribner). Names specific concrete things (Naples suits, Tokyo whisky bars, Bahamas fly fishing). Leads with community not content. Closes with "I'm glad you're here. Welcome to the game." Works because of pre-existing book credentials and five years of tenure.

**Key insight:** Coggins' page works because of external credentials. Your page has to work because of story and specificity — which is harder to fake and more compelling when done right.

## What the Current Page Gets Wrong (Specific Fixes)

1. **"Travel isn't just about destinations — it's about thresholds"** — too generic, sounds like every travel journal
2. **"Poetry and mythic fragments"** — not what the publication is. Remove.
3. **"I'll post 2–3 times a month"** — you publish daily. This destroys credibility.
4. **"Travelers seeking to awaken"** — generic spiritual travel copy, could be anyone
5. **"Join the community / fellowship of marginal pilgrims"** — weak CTA, no specificity
6. **Fernando Pessoa quote** — good, but it's doing all the heavy lifting for a page that isn't earning it
7. **No mention of four languages** — the most distinctive thing about the publication is invisible
8. **No mention of the origin story** — the coma, the return, the daughter and nephews — this is what makes the publication unlike anything else on Substack
